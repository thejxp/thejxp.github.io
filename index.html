Hi there!

I'm a junior at Cornell University studying Computer Science, working to use technology to further equity and inspire change. I'm particularly interested in projects that involve environmental advocacy, music analysis, and immigration trends and policy.

This past summer, I worked with Combplex, whose mission involves improving accessibility and understanding of data on bee health and developing comprehensive evaluations of beekeeper and farm quality using colony health metrics. My focus has been on using statistical models and neural networks to predict trends and detect anomalies in conditions, thereby giving beekeepers the tools to address issues as serious as a potential colony collapse.

I’ve also been involved with Women in Computing at Cornell for the past two years, most recently serving as VP of our community team, working to ensure that we remain committed to a mission of intersectionality and providing resources to those who need them most. During this time, I’ve organized workshops on combating bias and toxic work environments, overcoming mental health stigmas, and intersectionality and allyship in tech.

If you're interested in learning more about my work, drop me a line at jp2254[at]cornell.edu, or check out my links above. Thanks for stopping by!
